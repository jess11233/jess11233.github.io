Best Times for Dancing

The good thing about dance is that you can dance anytime you want!

Personally I practice dancing on the weekends, which is when I am most free.

The different times to practice and there benefits:

  • Morning: The morning is best for morning people who like to wake up in the morning. If you like having a good physical start to the day this is the best time to practice.
  • Weekends: The weekends are best for people who work all week long and want to relax once they get back from work. The weekend is also good for people who don't like to have a time restraint on how long they owuld like to practice, or for people who are not good at keeping track of time.
  • Evening: The evening is best for people who like to do things at night. This time at the day is most likely going to be the busiest time of the day for this activity so it is perfect for the people who enjoy the social aspect of dancing.

How to Start Dancing

There are many different way you can start dacning and it also depends on the type of dance you would like the learn.

The most common way to get into dancing is to join a dance class. Though this method can be very expensive and not always work for your schedule. Which is why I don't use this method of getting into dancing, I personally recommend first starting learning online on a free platform.

Here are some online learning platforms that are available to help you get started:

  • YouTube - A free platform offering countless dance tutorials across various styles, from beginner to professional levels.
  • STEEZY - A premium dance learning app with structured courses in hip-hop, popping, house, and other urban dance styles.
  • DancePlug - An online dance training platform offering classes in jazz, ballet, hip-hop, and contemporary.
  • Misty’s Dance Unlimited - An online dance academy providing high-quality lessons in ballet, tap, and lyrical dance.
  • Udemy - Offers dance courses for all levels, covering ballroom, salsa, belly dancing, and more.
